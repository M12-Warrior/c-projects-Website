Advertise & Paid Sponsorship

Reach professional truck drivers and fleet decision-makers who care about safety, wellness, and the road.

Our Audience

Mile 12 Warrior serves professional commercial motor vehicle drivers, owner-operators, fleet safety managers, and trucking-industry professionals. Our community is engaged with content on fatigue management, Hours of Service (HOS), wellness, mindset, safety checklists, and real-world tips from the cab. We attract drivers who want to stay sharp, compliant, and connected — making them a strong fit for brands that support the same values.

What We Offer

  • Banner and display advertising — Placements on high-traffic pages (home, blog, forum, course, shop).
  • Sponsored content — Native articles or posts that align with our editorial focus (safety, wellness, professional development), clearly labeled as sponsored.
  • Newsletter and email — Opportunities to reach subscribers when we introduce email offerings.
  • Custom partnerships — Long-term sponsorships, co-branded resources, or fleet-focused campaigns. Tell us your goals and we can tailor a package.

For rates, media kit, availability, and custom options, contact us at the address below.

Brand Fit

We welcome advertisers and sponsors whose products, services, or messaging align with our mission: trucking safety, driver wellness, professional development, and a supportive, professional community.

Strong fit: Fleet services and safety tech, driver wellness (nutrition, sleep, fitness), ELDs and compliance tools, insurance and financial services for drivers, trucking equipment and gear, training and CDL-related services, mental health and EAP resources, and brands that celebrate the profession without encouraging risky behavior.

We do not accept advertising that promotes unsafe driving, HOS violations, or content that conflicts with FMCSA safety values. We reserve the right to decline or remove any ad or sponsorship that does not meet our standards. Our audience’s trust comes first.

Disclosure

Paid and sponsored content on this site will be clearly labeled (e.g., “Sponsored,” “Ad,” or “Paid partnership”) in accordance with FTC guidelines (16 CFR Parts 255 and 435). We comply with our Terms of Service and Disclaimer in all advertising and sponsorship relationships.
